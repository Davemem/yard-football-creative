# BRAND.md

## Brand Position
Yard Football Creative should feel like a modern football organisation with a strong cultural point of view.
It should speak with confidence, warmth, clarity, and credibility.

## Brand Keywords
- grounded
- modern
- confident
- welcoming
- ambitious
- football-native
- developmental
- community-connected

## What It Must Not Feel Like
- generic startup
- generic junior sports club
- overdesigned agency template
- overly corporate institution
- aggressive elite-only brand
- vague empowerment language without substance

## Audience Groups
1. Girls and women interested in football development
2. Parents and families
3. Players seeking community participation
4. Players seeking elite progression
5. Coaches, collaborators, and partners

## Tone of Voice
- clear
- direct
- warm
- credible
- modern
- not cheesy
- not overhyped

## Copy Direction
Prefer:
- short, strong headlines
- grounded statements
- real football language
- clear calls to action

Avoid:
- empty inspirational slogans
- jargon-heavy coaching language
- corporate filler
- excessive exclamation marks

## Visual Direction
The brand should use:
- strong clean typography
- high-quality football imagery
- balanced use of space
- clear contrast
- restrained but confident colour usage
- subtle, earthy background tones that let content and calls to action stand forward

Imagery should prioritise:
- real football moments
- training environments
- women and girls in action
- community energy plus performance ambition

## Multi-Theme Brand Direction

Yard Football Creative may support multiple toggleable visual themes built from a library of approved logos in `assets/logo/`.
These themes should feel like different expressions of one brand system, not different brands competing with each other.

## Theme Rules

- every theme must still feel grounded, modern, confident, and football-native
- every theme must preserve clarity, trust, and usability first
- themes may shift emphasis, but must not become novelty skins
- the default theme should represent the clearest baseline version of the brand
- new themes should only be added when the logo asset is strong enough to support a coherent visual expression
- light and dark mode should feel like deliberate extensions of each theme, not generic system overlays

## What May Change Between Themes

- logo treatment
- accent colours
- supporting graphic devices
- background textures or patterns
- secondary type emphasis where readability remains strong
- light/dark surface balance, contrast treatment, and emphasis rules

## Background Colour Direction

Theme backgrounds should stay subtle and earthy so the content can pop out clearly.
Preferred background families include:
- greys
- muted greens
- dusty yellows
- warm browns
- other restrained natural tones that support the logo system

Backgrounds should support the content, not compete with it.
Stronger colour moments should be used more selectively for headings, accents, key surfaces, and calls to action.

Avoid:
- loud saturated full-page backgrounds
- overly bright colour blocking behind long-form content
- theme palettes that reduce text contrast or visual calm
- decorative colour use that distracts from the programme message

## What Must Stay Consistent Across Themes

- overall brand credibility
- page structure and content clarity
- call-to-action visibility
- accessibility standards
- tone of voice
- the sense that this is one Yard Football Creative website
- a clear and trustworthy experience in both light and dark mode

## Theme Review Standard

Any new theme should be reviewed by a human against these questions:
- does it still feel like Yard Football Creative?
- does it remain clear on mobile and desktop?
- does it support football credibility rather than visual noise?
- does it improve the experience, rather than just making it different?
- do both light and dark variants remain legible, intentional, and on-brand?
