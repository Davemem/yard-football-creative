If I close my eyes, I can still smell the wood fire burning, mixed with the fresh winter air in the middle of July. I can still see the sunsetting over the front paddock as my breathe puffs out a small, misty cloud in front of my eyes. I can still hear the birds twittering and my border collies barking, a car passing in the distance at the top of our slopped gravel driveway and the ever so soft sound of the fruit trees blowing in the light, cool breeze. I can hear my siblings giggle as they chase after a well-worn ball, followed by the roars of laughter that echo long into the night as they slip on the dewy freshly cut grass. Toes tingling, hands freezing but heart full of so much warmth.
